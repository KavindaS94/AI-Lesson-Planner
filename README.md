> An AI-powered SaaS tool that generates curriculum-aligned lesson plans, quizzes, and worksheets in minutes — saving teachers hours of prep time every week.

| Field | Details |

---

## 🎯 Problem

Teachers spend 6–10 hours per week on lesson planning and material creation. Most planning tools are either generic templates or complex LMS systems that require training. There is no fast, AI-native tool that understands curriculum standards and produces ready-to-use lesson plans in under a minute.

---

## 💡 Solution

A teacher inputs their **subject, grade level, topic, and duration** — and instantly receives a fully structured lesson plan including:

- Learning objectives
- Warm-up activity
- Main lesson activities
- Discussion questions
- Assessment quiz or worksheet
- Materials list

Everything is editable inline and exportable as a PDF or Google Doc.

## 🏗️ MVP Features (Build First)

### Phase 1 — Launch

- [ ] Lesson plan generator (subject + grade + topic + duration → full plan)

- [ ] Export as PDF and Google Doc

### Phase 2 — Growth

- [ ] Weekly lesson planner (auto-fill a full week, drag to reorder)
- [ ] Curriculum alignment (Common Core, IB, CBSE)
- [ ] Saved templates library

### Phase 3 — Moat

- [ ] Teacher community (share, remix, and rate lesson plans)
- [ ] Admin dashboard for school accounts
- [ ] LMS integrations (Google Classroom, Canvas)

## 💰 Pricing Strategy

| Tier | Price | Who | What's included |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Free | $0/month | New users | 5 plans/month, watermarked exports |
| Pro | $12/month | Individual teachers | Unlimited plans + quizzes, all export formats, curriculum alignment |
| School | $199/month | Up to 20 teachers | Everything in Pro + admin dashboard + priority support |
| District | Custom | 100+ teachers | Custom curriculum maps, SSO, LMS integrations, onboarding |

> **Strategy:** Start with individual teacher subscriptions to build revenue and social proof. Then upsell to schools organically once teachers adopt the tool and recommend it to their admin.

## 🚀 Go-to-Market: First 100 Users

### Week 1–2 — Direct outreach

- Message teachers in Facebook groups (e.g. Upper Elementary Teachers, Teachers Pay Teachers Community)
- Offer free beta access in exchange for feedback calls
- Target 50 outreach messages, aim for 10–15 beta testers

### Week 3–4 — Communities

- Post in r/Teachers, r/TeachersInTransition, r/education on Reddit
- Share in teacher Slack/Discord communities
- Frame it as a tool you built — teachers love supporting indie builders

### Month 2 — Influencer seeding

- Find 3–5 "TeacherTok" or Instagram educators (10k–100k followers)
- Offer lifetime free Pro access in exchange for an honest review post

### Month 3 — Referral loop

- Add a "share with a colleague" button in the app
- Teachers share resources constantly — a built-in referral loop can 3× growth rate

## ⚠️ Key Risks

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Big EdTech competition (Google, Khan Academy) | High | Niche down to one subject or curriculum first (e.g. IB Science, CBSE Maths) |
| Slow school procurement cycles (6–18 months) | Medium | Rely on individual subscriptions first; upsell schools once you have traction |
| Generic AI output quality | Medium | Invest heavily in prompt engineering; allow deep customisation per teacher |
| Teacher trust in AI | Low–Medium | Make editing easy; position as assistant not replacement |

---

## 🔑 Differentiation Strategy

Do not try to compete broadly. Pick **one niche first**:

- A single curriculum standard (e.g. IB only, or Common Core only)
- A single subject (e.g. Science or English Language)
- A specific geography (e.g. UK Key Stage 2 teachers)

Own that niche deeply before expanding. A big company cannot out-niche you.
